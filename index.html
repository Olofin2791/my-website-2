I am thrilled to share my journey into Frontend development.
For years, I dedicated my life to serving a higher calling as a priest in the Church of Nigeria (Anglican Communion), in the Diocese of Lagos West. It's a noble vocation filled with spiritual fulfilment and a sense of purpose. However, deep within, there was a burgeoning curiousity for technology and a desire to create meaningful digital experiences.

With unwavering faith and determination, I took the first steps towards this path. I started exploring HTML and CSS tutorials online, learning the basics of structuring web pages and styling elements to create visually appealing layouts.

My eagerness to learn made me enroll at innkeeper to master the fundamentals of programming and web development, understanding concepts like variables, functions, and control flow.
I studied design fundamentals, practiced wireframing and prototyping, and familiarized myself with design tools like Figma. I collaborated with peers and freelanced on web development gigs to gain practical experience and build my portfolio.

The journey of learning never ends in frontend development. I remain committed to continuous physical and spiritual growth and upskilling by staying updated with the latest industry trends, >attending workshops, and pursuing advanced courses in areas like responsive design, performance optimization, and accessibility.

As I continue to progress in my frontend journey, I'm passionate about giving back to the community by sharing my knowledge and mentoring aspiring developers. Whether through blog posts, tutorials, or mentoring programs, My mission remains the same: to inspire, uplift, and empower the next generation on their journey of faith and digital exploration.

Looking ahead, my goal is to continue pushing the boundaries of technology, contributing to innovative projects that make a positive impact on users' lives. I'm excited about the endless possibilities and opportunities for growth in this dynamic and ever-evolving field.
